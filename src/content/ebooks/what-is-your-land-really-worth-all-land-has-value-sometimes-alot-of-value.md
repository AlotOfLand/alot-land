**How land is valued — and why two similar parcels can be priced completely differently.** The single most important concept in land pricing is highest and best use: what is the most valuable thing this land can reasonably do? A parcel that can support a housing subdivision is worth far more than the same acreage with limited access — and you deserve to know which category your land falls into before you accept any offer.

**The three categories of land value** — buy-and-hold acreage, minor subdivide candidates (think mini ranches and small-lot splits), and major development land. Understanding which category your land fits helps you set realistic expectations and recognize a fair offer when you see one.

**What raises your land's value** — road access, utilities, favorable zoning, flat terrain, proximity to growth corridors, good soil, and clean title. And what can limit it — landlocked parcels, deed restrictions, wetlands, floodplains, steep slopes, rocky or clay-heavy soil. We walk through every factor clearly and honestly, including why even challenging land almost always has value worth uncovering.
